Eight Categories of Evidences that Joseph Smith was a prophet of God


Evidences that Joseph Smith was a prophet - Divine Protection and Miracles

Healings at Commerce, IL and Montrose, IO - On July 22, 1839, Joseph Smith healed all the saints in his house and yard that were displaced from Missouri. In company with Sidney Rigdon and several of the twelve, he went along the bank of the Mississippi River and healed all that were on the east side of the river. He then crossed the river to Montrose and healed many more there. ("Leaves From My Journal," Millennial Star, Oct. 10th & 17th, 1881, p. 647-671)

Gold Plates - Once it was known that Joseph Smith had gold plates, every means and strategy was used to get them from him, including searches of his home and possessions. Joseph was later able to show the plates to the eight witnesses, and the gold plates were shown by an angel to the three witnesses.

Spiritual gifts - Faith, prophecy, discernment, dreams, visions, tongues, heal and be healed, knowledge, wisdom, and communion with the heavens were all apparent throughout Joseph's life.

Elements tempered - Joseph was speaking in a grove outdoors when a rain and hail storm came. He asked the people to pray and continued preaching for 1 1/2 hours while the storm raged around them but not in the grove.

Indians understand English - Joseph was speaking to a group of American Indians through an interpreter. Joseph realized the interpreter was trying to turn the Indians against him. He sent the interpreter away and preached a sermon to them. The Indians understood every word.

Storm saved Zion's camp - When an armed group more than twice their size vowed to kill them, Joseph prophesied that God would save them; and they would not need to fight. While the saints rested safe in a Church, their enemies were driven and scattered by a violent hail storm.

Evidences that Joseph Smith was a prophet - Fruits

Never denied the first vision - Joseph Smith never denied the first vision although he was hated, imprisoned, and driven from three homes.

Preaching forgiveness after being tared and feathered - Joseph preached on forgiveness in his speech the day after being tared and feathered to a crowd that contained many men who had participated in the taring and feathering.

Thomas B. Marsh's forgiveness - Thomas B. Marsh was instrumental in getting the saints expelled from Jackson County and helped incite mobs against the church. When he asked Joseph Smith for forgiveness, Joseph readily forgave him.

Sorry $5 worth - When news came that someone's home had been burned down the night before, the discussion arose about how sorry everyone was for the brother. Joseph took out five dollars and said, "I feel sorry for this brother to the amount of five dollars; how much do you all feel sorry?" ("The Juvenile Instructor," Oct. 15, 1892)

Giving up Shoes to Sick Man - John Lyman Smith and his family moved to Nauvoo and were living in a stable made of logs until he could build himself a home. He and his family came down with fever. Joseph and Hyrum came and administered to the family promising "you shall all be well again". Upon leaving, Joseph placed his slippers upon John Smith's feet and rode home barefoot. The next day Joseph moved John Smith to his own home and nursed him until he recovered.("quoted in "Stories about Joseph Smith the Prophet: A Collection of Incidents Related by Friends who Knew Him", compiled by Edwin F. Parry [1934] p. 33-34)

Self criticism - D&C 5:21, 20:5, 24:1-2, 35:17-18 all admonish and warn Joseph about his sins and weaknesses.

Word of Wisdom - The Word of Wisdom is a law of health given to Joseph Smith by revelation in 1833. A recent study by a University in California showed that LDS High Priests are living an average of 9 1/2 years longer than other men in the population.

Welfare program - The church welfare program helps take care of the church's poor and needy.

Relief Society - Joseph Smith said that the church was not fully organized until the women had their own organization. In 1842, the Relief Society was organized and is now the largest woman's organization in the world.

Missionary work - Before the second coming of Christ, the gospel is to be taken to all the world. Today over 53,000 LDS missionaries are serving throughout the world.

Evidences that Joseph Smith was a prophet - Others Sharing Divine Experiences

Three witnesses - Three witnesses saw an angel and heard a voice declaring that the golden plates were the work of God.

All three witnesses left the church but never denied what they had seen and heard.

Eight witnesses - Eight witnesses saw and handled the gold plates.

Kirtland temple dedication - Many saw and heard heavenly choirs. Angels were seen by many on top of the temple, a pillar of fire rested on the temple all day, Joseph and Oliver Cowdery saw Christ and many ancient prophets inside the temple, people spoke and sang duets in tongues, many angels were seen inside the temple during the meetings, etc.

Priesthood ordinations - Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery were visited by John the Baptist and ordained to the Aaronic priesthood. Peter, James, and John appeared and restored the Melchizedek priesthood.

D&C sections - D&C 6&7 were also given to Oliver Cowdery. D&C 13 recounts the ordination of Joseph and Oliver Cowdery to the Aaronic Priesthood. D&C 110 recounts a vision given to Joseph and Oliver Cowdery in the Kirtland Temple. D&C 18 & 26 were also received by Oliver Cowdery and David Whitmer. D&C 35, 40, 44, 71, 76, and 100 were also received by Sidney Rigdon. D&C 42 was received in the presence of 12 elders. D&C 61 was given in response to a vision of William W. Phelps. D&C 137 begins with "The heavens were opened upon us."

Brigham appears to many as Joseph - After Joseph's death, a meeting was held to determine the future leadership of the church. Brigham Young sounded like, had the mannerisms of, and looked like Joseph Smith to many who were present.

Evidences that Joseph Smith was a prophet - Prophecies that have come True

Civil war - D&C 87:1 states that the civil war would begin in South Carolina over the slave question.

Hawn's Mill - Prior to the massacre, Joseph Smith told Jacob Hawn that it was not safe to remain in their settlement; and they needed to move closer to the other saints.

Willard Richards at the Martyrdom - Willard Richards was told, "You will stand where the balls will fly around you like hail, and men will fall dead by your side, and...there never shall a ball injure you." This came true when Joseph and Hyrum were killed at Carthage Jail.

Gov. Boggs shot - Joseph prophesied that Gov. Boggs, who had issued the Missouri Extermination Order, would die a violent death within a year. When Gov. Boggs was shot through his living room window a year later, the Missourians blamed Porter Rockwell and Joseph Smith. Porter Rockwell was captured but released eight months later for lack of evidence.

Porter Rockwell would get away honorably from the Missourians. This was fulfilled on Porter's release on Mar. 15, 1843. DHC 5:305 and "Teachings of the Prophet Joseph Smith," Section Six 1843-44, p. 285.

Porter Rockwell's hair - Porter Rockwell was promised that no bullet or knife would harm him if he did not cut his hair. He died a peaceful death at age 72 after living one of the most violent lives in the American West.

Joseph Smith's and Brigham Young's name - Joseph Smith's and Brigham Young's name had for good and evil among all men. Growth of the church - Joseph prophesied, "The church would fill north and south America--it will fill the world." Church membership now exceeds 13 million and adds more than 300,000 members each year

Saints move to Rocky Mountains - Part of Joseph Smith's journal entry on Aug. 6, 1842, stated: "I prophecy that the saints will continue to suffer much affliction and will be driven to the Rocky Mountains."

Joseph prophesied that the saints would build a great home in the Rockies.

Martyrdom - As Joseph was taken to Carthage Jail he said, "I am going like a lamb to the slaughter; but I am calm as a summer's morning; I have a conscience void of offense towards God, and towards all men. I shall die innocent, and it shall be yet said of me--he was murdered in cold blood." (D&C 135:4)

Jewish return to Israel - Joseph Smith had Orson Hyde go to the Holy Land and consecrate it for the return of the Jews in 1830.

Judge Stephen Douglas - Joseph Smith told Stephen Douglas that he would aspire to the presidency, but if he turned against Joseph or the Latter-day Saints he would fail. Stephen Douglas turned against the church and lost to an obscure backwoodsman, Abraham Lincoln. Stephen Douglas only got 12 electoral votes.
